Frequently Asked Questions

General Information

Q: Where is Cossatot River State Park located?
A: Cossatot River State Park is located in the Ouachita mountain country, just southeast of Mena, Arkansas.

Q: How long is the Cossatot River?
A: The Cossatot River flows for approximately 26 miles before its current ceases at Gillham Lake.

Camping and Accommodations

Q: What types of camping sites are available at Cossatot River State Park?
A: Tent sites (no hookups) are available at the Cossatot Falls Area, Sandbar Area, and Ed Banks Area. A primitive group camping area is also available by reservation at River Ridge Group Camp.

Q: Are there any amenities available at the campsites?
A: Yes, the campsites have access to restrooms, picnic sites, and river access. The River Ridge Group Camp also has a pavilion, fire pit, grill, and restroom.

Recreation and Activities

Q: What types of recreational activities are available at Cossatot River State Park?
A: Visitors can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, hiking, and wildlife observation. The park also offers interpretive programs year-round.

Q: Are the rapids on the Cossatot River suitable for beginners?
A: No, the rapids on the Cossatot River are Class IV/Class V and are not recommended for casual canoeists or beginners.

Visitor Center and Amenities

Q: What amenities are available at the visitor center?
A: The visitor center features exhibits, a wildlife observation room, meeting room, and gift shop.

Q: Are there any picnic sites available at Cossatot River State Park?
A: Yes, picnic sites are available at the Brushy Creek Recreation Area.

Q: Can I reserve a group camping area at Cossatot River State Park?
A: Yes, the River Ridge Group Camp is available by reservation.

Q: What does the name "Cossatot" mean?
A: The name "Cossatot" is an alteration of the French "cassé-tête," which means "crushed/broken head/skull."
